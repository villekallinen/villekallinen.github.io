Eye Tracking Applications in Driver Monitoring and Esports

This Bachelor's thesis presents a literature review of eye-tracking technology and its applications in driver fatigue detection and esports performance analysis.

The thesis examines physiological indicators derived from eye movements, evaluates modern eye-tracking systems and reviews current research on how gaze behaviour can be used to assess human performance, attention and fatigue.

Project Overview

Eye tracking provides a non-invasive method for measuring visual attention, fatigue and cognitive workload. Advances in camera technology, image processing and computer vision have made eye tracking an increasingly important tool in transportation safety, healthcare and human–computer interaction.

This literature review investigates how eye-movement characteristics—including blink behaviour, gaze direction, fixations and eye closure—can be used to monitor driver alertness and evaluate player behaviour and performance in competitive esports.

Driver Monitoring

Driver fatigue can reduce reaction speed, situational awareness and decision-making ability. Eye-tracking systems can monitor indicators such as blink frequency, blink duration, eyelid closure and changes in gaze behaviour to detect reduced alertness.

The reviewed studies showed that eye-based indicators can provide valuable information about driver fatigue. However, their reliability can be affected by individual differences, lighting, head movement, eyewear and the measurement environment.

Eye Tracking in Esports

In esports, gaze measurements can be used to investigate how players distribute visual attention, identify relevant information and react to rapidly changing situations. Fixation patterns, gaze transitions and areas of interest can reveal differences in visual strategies between players.

Eye-tracking data can therefore support performance analysis, training and research into decision-making and cognitive workload in competitive gaming.

Key Findings

The reviewed studies demonstrate that eye tracking is a promising technology for detecting driver fatigue and evaluating human performance. Measures such as blink rate, eye-closure duration, gaze patterns and fixation behaviour provide valuable information about attention, fatigue and cognitive state.

The review also highlighted that no single eye-based indicator is sufficient under all conditions. More reliable monitoring can be achieved by combining multiple eye-movement measures or integrating eye tracking with other physiological and behavioural signals.

Future Work

Future developments in artificial intelligence, computer vision and multimodal sensing are expected to improve the reliability and accessibility of eye-tracking systems. Combining eye-movement measurements with additional physiological and behavioural data could support more robust applications in driver monitoring, healthcare, esports and human–computer interaction.

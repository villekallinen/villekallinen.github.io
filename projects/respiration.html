Respiratory Rate Estimation from Smartphone Accelerometer Data

Development of an algorithm that estimates respiratory rate from three-axis accelerometer data recorded with a smartphone placed on the chest and validates the result against a BIOPAC MP150 respiratory belt.

The challenge

Respiration causes subtle chest movement that can be detected by a smartphone accelerometer. The respiratory component is weak and may be masked by measurement noise, body motion, sensor orientation and non-uniform sampling.

The goal is to build a robust signal-processing pipeline that extracts respiratory information and estimates respiratory rate under different measurement conditions.

My contribution

  • Designed and implemented the signal-processing pipeline in MATLAB.
  • Developed preprocessing for non-uniformly sampled accelerometer data.
  • Implemented PCA-based respiratory signal extraction.
  • Developed autocorrelation-based cycle detection and adaptive peak detection.
  • Designed confidence metrics for respiratory-rate estimates.
  • Compared the estimates with BIOPAC reference measurements.

Signal-processing pipeline

01Import and synchronise data
02Interpolate and filter
03Extract signal with PCA
04Detect cycles with ACF and peaks
05Estimate rate and confidence

Results

The current pipeline extracts a respiration-related signal from three-axis accelerometer measurements and produces respiratory-rate estimates for comparison with the reference belt. Final quantitative validation results will be added after the complete dataset has been analysed.

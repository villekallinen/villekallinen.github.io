Eye Tracking Applications in Driver Monitoring and Esports

This Bachelor's thesis presents a literature review on eye-tracking technology and its applications in driver fatigue detection and esports performance analysis. The thesis examines physiological indicators derived from eye movements, evaluates modern eye-tracking systems and reviews current research on how gaze behaviour can be used to assess human performance, attention and fatigue.

Project Overview

Eye tracking provides a non-invasive method for measuring visual attention, fatigue and cognitive workload. During the last decade, advances in camera technology and computer vision have enabled eye tracking to become an important tool in transportation safety, healthcare and human-computer interaction.

This literature review investigates how eye movement characteristics, such as blink behaviour, gaze direction, fixations and eye closure, can be utilized for monitoring driver alertness and evaluating player performance in competitive esports.

Key Findings

The reviewed studies demonstrate that eye tracking is a promising technology for detecting driver fatigue and evaluating human performance. Measures such as blink rate, eye closure duration, gaze patterns and fixation behaviour provide valuable information about attention and cognitive state.
